# Licence

## Summary

| What | Licence |
|---|---|
| The specification text, documentation, diagrams and prose | Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) |
| Schemas, reference implementations, test suites, deployment templates and other code | Apache License 2.0 |
| The name "The Cobalt Book" and associated marks | Reserved — see Section 3 |

Two licences are used deliberately. CC BY 4.0 is the right instrument for a standard that must be quoted, adapted and embedded in departmental and supplier documentation. It is the wrong instrument for code, because it grants no patent licence and was never drafted for software. Apache 2.0 covers the executable half and grants the patent licence that implementers need in order to build without commercial risk.

## 4. Conformance claims

Conformance is a claim about a system, made publicly and checkably. It is not a licence grant and it is not certified by anyone.

A conformance claim is valid only where:

1. a machine-readable declaration exists and is published, or is available to the party relying on the claim;
2. the conformance level claimed (C1, C2 or C3) is stated explicitly, rather than the unqualified word "conformant"; and
3. for C2 and C3, the corresponding test results or rehearsal record are available to the party relying on the claim.

"Cobalt compliant" as an unqualified marketing statement is not a conformance claim and should be treated as meaningless by anyone assessing it.

No fee is charged for making a conformance claim, and no party is authorised to charge one. Any offer to sell Cobalt certification, accreditation or approved-supplier status is unauthorised, and readers are invited to say so publicly.
